Marc Rhodes

Slide 01

You Didn't Come This Far By Accident

What got you here was not luck.

It was a decision to tolerate discomfort that most people walk away from.

That is worth acknowledging before we go any further.

Slide 02

What Is The First Mountain

The Climb

The first mountain is the individual pursuit of success, status and achievement.

The Drive

You claw your way toward it. Accomplishment is the main point.

The Question

Most people spend their entire lives climbing it without asking if it is actually theirs.

Slide 03

The Culture That Sends You Up It

From the moment you could understand language, your worth was tied to your output.

Get the grades. Build the business. Be seen to be winning.

Nobody asked whether the mountain you were told to climb was actually yours.

Slide 04

Who You Are On The Way Up

Relentless. Restless. Regularly wrong about what it costs.

Your self worth is almost entirely tied to results.

The inner voice is loud, demanding and rarely kind.

Exercise

Not what you built. What you gave up to build it.

Most people have never said this out loud.

Slide 06

The Identity You Build On The Way Up

Your beliefs inform your behaviours. Your behaviours become your identity.

On the first mountain, you slowly become the business.

That is useful up to a point. Then it becomes a trap.

Slide 07

The Cost On Your Relationships

The people closest to you feel the cost before you do.

You are physically present but mentally somewhere else entirely.

They have been quietly adjusting their expectations of you for years.

Slide 08

Forgiving Yourself

What they talk about at your funeral.

Knowing this now is the advantage.

Session 2

The Good, The Bad And
The Ugly Side Of Leadership

Ben Hawksworth

11:00am - 12:00pm

Redefining Leadership

Leadership Is Not What
You Think It Is

Most of what you were told about leadership is inaccurate.

Leadership is not a title. It is not a skill set you acquire once.

It is a daily decision about who you are going to be in relation to the people around you.

The Truth About Most Leaders

Most people in leadership positions are not there because they are the best leaders.

They are there because they were the most ambitious or the last person standing.

Leadership is demonstrated under pressure. Not during the easy periods.

What Great Leadership Looks Like

Heard

Great leadership makes people feel genuinely listened to

Trusted

It creates psychological safety without making things soft

Capable

People are inspired, supported and resourced to do their best work

Your role is to ensure the people around you are inspired, supported and resourced.

Active Listening As A Leadership Tool

Most leaders listen only so they can respond.
That is not listening. That is waiting with your mouth closed.

Knowledge is not power. Implementation is power.

Exercise

The Best Leader
You Ever Had

Think of the leader who actually made you better.
Not the most successful one.

What did they do specifically? How did they make you feel?

Share that with the person next to you.

The Bad

Lazy Leadership

Telling people what to do instead of developing them gets short term compliance.

It creates long term dependency. Then the leader wonders why nobody uses their initiative.

Those two things are directly connected.

The Seven Deadly Habits

Demanding Criticising Blaming Complaining Threatening Punishing Rewarding to Control

Every person in this room has used at least one of these this week.

They feel effective in the short term. They destroy trust over time.

The Ugly

Authoritarian Leadership

Shouting at people is not leadership.
It is an insecurity wearing a title.

The person who leads through fear is not respected. They are endured.

There is a significant difference between those two things.

The Passive Aggressive Middle Ground

Passive aggression is equally destructive and considerably more corrosive.

It creates an atmosphere nobody can name but everyone can feel.

It is the result of not developing the skill or the courage to communicate directly.

Exercise

Which Habit
Shows Up In You?

Which of the seven deadly habits showed up most in the last thirty days?

Be honest. Not with the room. With yourself.

That answer is your starting point.

Assertive Communication As The Standard

Not Passive

Assertive

Not Aggressive

Honest but not hurtful. Direct but not damaging.

The mantra is simple. Do no harm.

Conflict As A Leadership Skill

Avoiding conflict is not leadership.
It is the abdication of it.

The micro grievances stack up and the explosion is always worse than the conversation you avoided.

Initiating conflict well is a skill. It requires courage, preparation and genuine care.

Understanding Your Conflict Origin

The way you handle conflict under pressure was shaped before you could choose it.

Understanding your conflict origin creates a moment of choice that was not available before.

That moment between stimulus and response is where your leadership actually lives.

Exercise

The Conversation You
Have Been Avoiding

Think of one professional conversation you have been putting off.

Not because it is irrelevant. Because it is uncomfortable.

What is the actual cost of continuing to avoid it?

Taking Your Ego Out Of The Equation

Very few people around you are operating on the same level of urgency as you.

Expecting them to think like you is a guaranteed path to frustration for everyone.

The leader who takes their ego out of the equation builds genuine loyalty. Not compliance.

The Leader As Servant

Your role is to ensure people feel trusted, supported and free to do their best work.

If you are not getting the outcomes you want, that is a leadership problem before it is a people problem.

The results your team produces are the direct output of the leadership they are receiving.

What Your People Actually Need

Attention

They need to know you are paying attention. To them. Not just the results.

Trust

They need to feel trusted enough to take risks without fear of punishment.

Consistency

They need you to be consistent. Not just on the good days.

Communicating Through Pressure

Your real communication standard is not what you demonstrate when things are easy.

It is what you demonstrate when the deadline is close and someone makes a mistake.

Pressure reveals character. For better or for worse.

Exercise

The Best Version Of
Your Leadership

Not the version you perform for an audience.
The version you are at your absolute best.

What does that look like specifically?

What conditions allow that version of you to show up consistently?

Honest Feedback As A Gift

If people around you are not telling you the truth, you are operating blind.

The most likely reason is that at some point you made it unsafe to do so.

The standard for honest feedback starts with you.

The Cost Of Not Developing As A Leader

Most high performers do not leave bad companies.

They leave leaders who made them feel undervalued, unseen or unable to grow.

You will never get a resignation letter that says this. But the data is clear.

Exercise

What Would Your
Team Say?

If your team were completely honest with no professional consequence.

What would they say about what it is like to be led by you?

Write the parts you suspect are true but would rather not think about.

Leadership As An Endurance Event

Leadership is not a phase you go through on the way to something else.

It is a lifelong practice of developing the emotional capacity to get the best from people.

The leaders who last are the ones who committed to the person behind the title.

What Separates Good From Great

Good Leaders

Produce results through people.

Great Leaders

Produce results through people and leave those people better than they found them.

Every crisis is a leadership examination. The great ones pass it by being more human. Not less.

The Standard You Set Today

The people who depend on you deserve a version of you who has thought seriously about this.

You have been honest about where your current standard is. That is not a small thing.

What are you going to do differently when you walk out of this room?

Session 3

The Valley Of Despair

Everything You Need To Know About Being On Your Second Mountain

Speaker: Marc Rhodes

1:00pm - 2:00pm

The Starting Point

You Made It. Now What.

There is a moment when the goal you have been chasing finally arrives.

And instead of the clarity you expected, something unsettling shows up instead.

That moment has a name. And understanding it changes everything.

The Valley Of Despair

The valley sits between the two mountains.

It is where you arrive when the first mountain stops delivering what it promised.

It is disorienting precisely because you did everything right.

Why The First Mountain Stops Being Enough

The first mountain promised that success would produce peace.

That achievement would produce meaning.

Those promises are incomplete. Not false. Incomplete.

The Identity Crisis In The Valley

If your identity became the business, the valley asks who you are without it.

That is genuinely difficult work. Most high achievers are not prepared for it.

The ones who lean in discover something remarkable on the other side.

Exercise

Have You Been In The Valley?

Think of a time you achieved something significant and felt unexpectedly empty.

When the goal arrived and the fulfilment did not.

That experience has a name now.

What Is The Second Mountain

From

Self centred

to

Other centred

From

Independence

to

Interdependence

From

Accumulation

to

Commitment

It is not retirement. It is the highest performance available to a human being.

The Four Commitments

Vocation

A commitment to a vocation that is genuinely yours.

Relationships

A commitment to relationships of real depth.

Community

A commitment to a community you are building rather than just occupying.

Resume Virtues Revisited

You spent the first mountain building what you bring to the marketplace.

The second mountain asks you to build the other side of the ledger.

Neither is more important. A full life requires both.

The Vocation Question

A vocation is not a job.

It is work that aligns with who you actually are at your core.

On the first mountain you build competence.

On the second mountain you ask whether the thing you built your competence in is actually yours.

Exercise

What Would You Do If You Already Had Enough?

If the financial pressure was removed entirely, what would you be doing?

Not retirement. What work would you choose because it genuinely matters to you?

That answer is a compass bearing.

From Independence To Interdependence

The first mountain celebrates the self made person. The solo achievement.

That narrative is both culturally dominant and profoundly misleading.

Nobody builds anything of real value entirely alone.

What Happens If You Ignore The Valley

Most people accelerate harder into the first mountain when the valley arrives.

Or they numb it. With work, with distraction, with comfort.

The valley does not respond to avoidance. It just waits.

Cognitive Dissonance In The Valley

You want a bigger life and you cannot let go of the habits that belong to the old one.

The resistance is not a sign that change is wrong.

It is a sign that the change is real.

Exercise

What Are You Holding On To?

Professional

One thing in your professional life you know needs to change but have been avoiding.

Personal

One thing in your personal life.

What is the actual cost of holding on to both of them for another year?

Suffering Your Way To Wisdom

The valley is genuinely difficult. That is the honest version.

Everyone who has navigated it honestly came out with a clarity that cannot be acquired any other way.

You cannot climb your way to it. It has to be lived.

You Cannot Climb Out Alone

The valley requires community, honest relationship and the willingness to be helped.

The founders who cross it fastest are not the most capable ones in isolation.

They are the ones who had the humility to tell the truth to at least one other person.

The Second Mountain And The Business

This does not mean walking away from the business.

It means bringing a completely different quality of intention to it.

When the founder transitions to the second mountain the business changes too.

Relationships On The Second Mountain

On the second mountain, relationships are commitments. Not networks.

They are built on shared experience, genuine vulnerability and the evidence of showing up over time.

That cannot be rushed and it cannot be manufactured.

Exercise

What Does Your Second Mountain Look Like?

Not the version you would put in a public post. The honest version.

What commitments would need to be in place?

What would you need to let go of to get there?

The Four Commitments In Practice

Vocation

The work that is actually aligned with who you are.

Relationships

Fewer connections held with genuine depth and presence.

Community

What you are giving to something larger than yourself.

Freedom From vs Freedom To

First Mountain

Freedom from

Scarcity. Insecurity. External pressure.

Second Mountain

Freedom to

What are you now free to do?

The deeper the commitment, the freer you actually become.

Exercise

The Eulogy Test

What would you want said about you when the time comes?

Not your achievements. What you were actually like.

Running that test now gives you the chance to change the answer while you still can.

The Valley Is Not The Destination

The valley is a crossing. Not a residence.

It is asking you to expand beyond who you have been so far.

Most people are not prepared to do that. You now have the framework.

What Changes And What Stays

What Stays

The ambition, the drive, the commitment to the highest level.

What Changes

The why behind the effort.

From proving something to contributing something.

The Second Mountain Is Yours To Claim

It is not a reward for having climbed the first one perfectly.

It is available to every person in this room. Right now.

The only thing standing between you and it is the decision to pursue it.

Session 4

Personal vs Professional
Problem Solving

Ben Hawksworth

2:00pm - 3:00pm

This Session Is Different

There is no passive listening here.

Everything from this point is participatory.

What you take away is directly proportional to what you put in.

The Two Domains

Professional Problem Solving

Clear problem. Clear goal. Decision. Action.

Personal Problem Solving

Messier. More intimate. Almost entirely emotional.

The personal problems are almost always the root cause of the professional ones.

How High Achievers Approach
Personal Problems

Most deal with personal problems by working harder professionally.

It is the most available anaesthetic.

The personal problem does not go away during this period. It compounds.

The Circle of Control

Inside Your Control

Your thoughts. Your communication. Your standards. Your response.

Inside Your Influence

Your team's performance. The people closest to you.

Outside Your Control

Stop fighting for it. Entirely.

Exercise

Mapping the Problem

Personal

Write your three most significant personal problems.

Professional

Write your three most significant professional problems.

Mark each one: inside your control, inside your influence, or outside your control entirely.

Reaction vs Response

Reactive

Fires back immediately from emotion.

Responsive

Takes the split second between stimulus and action.

That split second is where your character lives.

Root Cause vs
Presenting Problem

The presenting problem is rarely the actual problem.

The person who says their problem is time management is usually dealing with an inability to set boundaries.

The surface is visible. The root cause is underneath it.

Exercise

The Root Cause Question

Take each of your six problems and ask why three times in sequence.

By the third answer you are usually close to the root cause.

You are not looking for the answer that sounds good. You are looking for the one that is true.

The Same Person.
Two Different Standards.

Professional You

Approaches a business problem with calm, structured thinking.

Personal You

Clouds over because the emotional stakes are higher.

The goal is to bring the same quality of thinking to both.

Exercise

Say It Out Loud

Find the person in the room you have spoken to least today.

Share one professional problem and one personal problem. One sentence each.

Notice how it feels to say these things out loud to someone you barely know.

The Cost of Unresolved
Personal Problems

Every unresolved personal problem is paying rent inside your head during your working day.

These are not separate from your professional performance.

The founder carrying significant unresolved personal problems is not operating at full capacity.

What Most People Do Instead

Most people in the face of a significant personal problem find something productive to do instead.

Go to the gym

Schedule a meeting

Check their phone

It feels like action. It is movement in the opposite direction.

The Problem Solving Framework

1

Define the Root Cause

The honest version. Not the presenting problem.

2

Circle of Control

Identify what is fully inside your control within this problem.

3

Single Next Action

Define the single next action with a specific date attached to it.

Exercise

Apply the Framework

Take the most significant problem from your personal list. The one sitting there longest.

Apply the three steps: Root cause. Circle of control. Single next action.

Leave this exercise with something specific and time bound.

The Personal Standard That
Underpins Everything

The personal standards you hold yourself to in private are the foundation of your professional performance.

A plan is only as useful as the emotional security of the person implementing it.

The founders who take this seriously produce results others cannot explain or replicate.

Exercise

What Standard Are You
Holding Yourself To?

Professional Standard

Write the standard you expect of yourself professionally. Current. Not aspirational.

Personal Standard

Write the standard you hold yourself to in your personal life.

Look at the gap. That gap is the work.

Solving for Both Simultaneously

The idea that you must choose between a great career and a great personal life is a myth.

The people who appear to have both are not lucky. They are deliberate.

They engineered the conditions rather than hoping balance would arrive.

The Accountability Structure

Knowing what to do is not the same as doing it.

The gap between insight and action is closed by accountability.

Not the abstract kind. The specific relational kind where someone will actually ask you.

Exercise

The Commitment

Professional Commitment

Something specific with a completion date.

Personal Commitment

Something specific with a completion date.

Tell the person next to you. Ask them to check in with you in thirty days.

The Window of Opportunity

There is a window of energy and clarity that opens at an event like this one.

It is real. It is also temporary.

Take one specific action before you leave this building today. Not tomorrow.

What Integration
Actually Looks Like

Integration is not balance.

It means the same person showing up with integrity in both the personal and professional.

That is built through daily practice. Not a weekend decision.

Exercise

The Final Reflection

Write down the single most important thing you heard today.

The one thing that if acted on consistently over twelve months would make the biggest difference.

That is the thread worth following.

What You Take Home

A language for where you are, where you are going and what the actual work is.

Three frameworks you can use tomorrow morning in the first difficult moment of your day.

Not theory. Operational.

The Work Continues

Nothing said today is a destination. It is a starting point.

Pick one thing. Commit to it with every resource you have.

Do not try to implement everything. Just start.

The Door Is Open

The second mountain is something you choose.

The valley is somewhere you pass through. Not somewhere you live.

Walk out of here knowing what you are going to do differently. Tomorrow morning.
